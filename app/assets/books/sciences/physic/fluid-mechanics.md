## Specific weight γ
it depends on the value of the gravitational acceleration g

γ = G/V = m⋅g/V = ρ⋅g
G [N] – weight
V [m³] – volume
m [kg] – mass
g [m/s²] – gravitational acceleration, varies with altitude and latitude.

## Density - Volumetric mass density
Ratio between mass (m) and volume (V)
ρ=m/V [kg/m³] 

Ex: Gasoline density is [720 - 770] kg/m³
745 kg/m³, 55 liters (0.055 m³) → m = ρ⋅V = 745 ⋅ 0.055 = 40.975 kg

### Volumic thermal expansion
Density is not constant when temperatures vary. The dependency of density function of temperature is:

ρ=ρT0/1+α⋅ΔT

ρT0 [kg/m³] – density at a reference temperature T0
α [1/°C] – thermal expansion coefficient of the liquid at temperature T0
ΔT [°C] – temperature difference. ΔT=T–T0
T0 [°C] – reference temperature
T [°C] – current temperature

Water density at 20 °C = 998.2071 kg/m³
The thermal expansion coefficient for water is 69·10-6 1/K at 20 °C
Density of water at 80 °C:
ΔT = (80-20)+273.15 = 60+273.15=333.15 K
ρ=998.2071/1+69⋅10−6⋅333.15 = 975.78 kg/m³
